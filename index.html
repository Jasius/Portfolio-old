About me

Hi, my name is Mindaugas Jasiulis, I am a  years old self taught front-end developer who currently lives in Lithuania.

Best experience to me as a front-end developer was on Reddit community forum /r/Windows10 simply due to its big audience which allowed me to see how effective some of the front-end techniques such as color coding are. Content structuring, color coding helped content engagement and interaction grow significantly. I'm happy that my work reaches over 1 million unique users every month on Windows community alone while on /r/Olympics community during 2016 Olympic games my work was seen by 3 million unique users and viewed 22 million times. I've received job offers from people that loved my work on Windows Community. Thanks to this side project, I have had plenty of interaction with Microsoft engineers, prominent developers and lots of other interesting people.

Apart from Web development related work I was involved in translating websites such as OneFootball into Lithuanian and Russian languages.

Projects

Windows Community

2016

My main task on this community forum was to increase community interaction and come up with a way to highlight certain content which people enjoy. I've managed to do that by enforcing flairs system with a help of an automated bot and automatically highlighting threads based on their flairs so it's easier to distinguish content.

Bookie.GG

2015

Bookie.GG is a gambling site on which you were able to bet Counter-Strike: Global Offensive weapon skins, which can be traded and sold on Steam market or third party services. CS:GO skins hold monetary value on the market, so betting took off really quickly once skins were introduced to the game.

Invisibars extension

2017

Invisibars is a Chrome extension which removes wasteful scrolls bars to give you more real estate while browsing the web. It is a simple extension with very low footprint and unlike other similar extensions Invisibars does not break custom modals and scrollbars on websites.

ClearShift

2015

ClearShift is a site for the promotion of fourth major version of my Reddit theme which I originally made for /r/MicrosoftBand community. I put a lot of hours into it, so I wanted to outsource it and make it easy to customize, set up with pre-set code snippets so even people with little to no CSS knowledge can use ClearShift and customize it to their own needs. Furthermore, broadening the reach of my ClearShift theme helps me improve it even further with the due to feedback and bug reports. Moreover, I'm using this site to promote other subreddits that use ClearShift so people can see how it looks in action.

During 2016 Olympic games my theme was being used on /r/Olympics community so it theme was seen by 3 million unique users and was viewed 22 million times for the duration of the 2016 Olympics.
